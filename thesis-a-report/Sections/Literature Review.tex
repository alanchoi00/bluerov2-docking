\section{Literature Review}

\subsection{Existing Work in AUV/ROV Docking}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Surveys of docking strategies for AUVs and ROVs: mechanical, acoustic, vision-based
    \item Notable AUV docking demonstrations: seabed or moored docking stations, dedicated infrastructure
    \item ROV docking examples and industrial practices (inspection vehicles, ROV launch/recovery systems)
    \item Comparison of autonomous (AUV) vs tele-operated (ROV) docking research outcomes
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Vision-Based Docking Techniques}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Fiducial markers: AprilTag, ArUco, custom passive markers and their robustness underwater
    \item Active markers and beacons: LED arrays, modulated light sources, strobe methods
    \item Learning-based approaches: CNNs for detection and segmentation of docking targets
    \item Motion/optical-flow and classical feature tracking for relative motion estimation
    \item Image enhancement and dehazing methods tailored for underwater imagery
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Other Sensing Methods and Hybrid Systems}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Acoustic positioning and homing (USBL, DVL-assisted approaches) and their range/accuracy tradeoffs
    \item Magnetic homing and mechanical alignment aids
    \item Sensor fusion strategies combining vision with acoustic, inertial, or range sensors
    \item Practical hybrid systems used in industry for robust localization
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Known Limitations in Academic Research}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Many studies assume static docks or seabed-mounted stations rather than free-floating platforms
    \item Experiments often conducted in clear-water laboratory tanks, not turbid field conditions
    \item Focus on AUV platforms with different constraints compared to ROVs (e.g., velocity profiles, payload)
    \item Limited work addressing real-time turbidity adaptation or onboard compute constraints
\end{itemize}

\subsection{ROV-Specific Challenges}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Tether dynamics influence vehicle motion and can interfere with docking approaches
    \item Control latency and operator-in-the-loop behaviors in semi-autonomous systems
    \item Payload and power constraints for additional sensors and processors onboard ROVs
    \item Integration challenges with commercial ROV platforms and safety requirements
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Research Gap}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Lack of integrated systems handling free-floating docks with significant relative motion
    \item Sparse research on turbidity-adaptive vision pipelines validated on ROV platforms
    \item Few demonstrations combining fiducial/active markers, image enhancement, and control for docking
    \item Need for end-to-end validation: detection, pose estimation, control, and engagement under realistic conditions
\end{itemize}

\subsection{How This Project Fits Into the Literature}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Positioning the project as addressing free-floating dock dynamics + turbidity adaptation on ROV hardware
    \item Building on fiducial-based and learning-based detection approaches with targeted robustness improvements
    \item Aiming to provide experimental validation bridging tank tests and field-like turbidity scenarios
    \item Contribution: practical system design, evaluation metrics, and recommendations for ROV docking deployments
\end{itemize}
